# Testimonial Structure

## Overview

Real or realistic customer shares their experience with product. Builds trust through authentic storytelling.

---

## Structure

### Part 1: Introduction (20%)
Who is the person, establish credibility

### Part 2: Problem (25%)
What challenge they faced

### Part 3: Discovery (15%)
How they found the product

### Part 4: Experience (25%)
Using the product, benefits

### Part 5: Result (15%)
Current state, recommendation

---

## For 30-Second Ad

```yaml
Introduction (6s):
  - Name, occupation, location
  - "Hi, I'm Sarah, a busy mom of three"

Problem (7s):
  - Their specific challenge
  - "I was always exhausted, no time for myself"

Discovery (5s):
  - How they found product
  - "Then I discovered [Product]"

Experience (7s):
  - Using product, benefits
  - "Now I use it every morning..."

Result (5s):
  - Current state, recommendation
  - "I feel amazing. You should try it too"
  - Logo
```

---

## Variations

### 1. Multiple Testimonials
```yaml
Person_1 (8s): Quick testimonial
Person_2 (8s): Quick testimonial
Person_3 (8s): Quick testimonial
Product_Shot (6s): Logo + CTA
```

### 2. Journey Format
```yaml
Before (10s): Life before product
Transition (5s): Trying product
After (10s): Life after product
Recommendation (5s): "Try it yourself"
```

### 3. Interview Style
```yaml
Question_1 (7s): "What was your biggest challenge?"
Answer_1 (7s): Customer responds
Question_2 (7s): "How did [Product] help?"
Answer_2 (7s): Customer responds
Closing (2s): Logo
```

---

## Visual Approach

### Authentic Style
```yaml
camera: Handheld or gimbal, natural movement
lighting: Natural, realistic
location: Real environment (home, office, etc)
framing: Medium close-up, eye contact with camera
style: Documentary, genuine
```

### Polished Style
```yaml
camera: Locked off or smooth movement
lighting: Professional, flattering
location: Clean, controlled environment
framing: Well-composed, rule of thirds
style: Commercial, aspirational
```

---

## Credibility Elements

### 1. Specific Details
```yaml
good: "I lost 15 pounds in 8 weeks"
bad: "I lost a lot of weight"
```

### 2. Before/After
```yaml
approach: Show actual transformation
visual: Side-by-side or sequential
```

### 3. Real Names and Locations
```yaml
format: "Sarah M., Portland, OR"
effect: Builds trust, feels authentic
```

### 4. Occupation/Context
```yaml
format: "John, Teacher and Father of Two"
effect: Helps audience relate
```

## Examples

### Example 1: Fitness Product (30s)
```yaml
S1 (5s): "I'm Mike, 42, from Chicago"
S2 (6s): "I was out of shape, no energy"
S3 (4s): "My doctor recommended [Product]"
S4 (8s): Mike using product, working out
S5 (4s): "Lost 20 pounds, feel 10 years younger"
S6 (3s): Product logo + "Start your journey"
```

### Example 2: App (30s)
```yaml
S1 (4s): "Hi, I'm Lisa, small business owner"
S2 (6s): "Managing everything was overwhelming"
S3 (5s): "Found [App] and everything changed"
S4 (8s): Lisa using app, showing features
S5 (4s): "Now I'm organized and stress-free"
S6 (3s): App logo + "Try free for 30 days"
```

### Example 3: Multiple Quick Testimonials (30s)
```yaml
S1 (8s): Person 1 - "Changed my life" + quick story
S2 (8s): Person 2 - "Best decision ever" + quick story
S3 (8s): Person 3 - "Wish I found it sooner" + quick story
S4 (6s): Product montage + logo + "Join thousands of happy customers"
```

## Authenticity Markers

### What Makes It Feel Real

```yaml
natural_speech:
  - Slight pauses, "um", "you know"
  - Not overly scripted
  - Conversational tone

body_language:
  - Natural gestures
  - Genuine smiles
  - Eye contact

environment:
  - Real locations
  - Lived-in spaces
  - Not overly staged

emotion:
  - Genuine feeling
  - Not over-acted
  - Relatable reactions
```

## When to Use

**Best for**:
- Building trust
- New products
- Skeptical audiences
- High-consideration purchases
- Health/wellness products
- Services
- Subscription products
- B2B solutions

**Characteristics**:
- Authentic voice
- Relatable stories
- Social proof
- Trust-building
- Emotional connection

## Production Tips

### 1. Real Customers vs Actors

**Real Customers**:
```yaml
pros: Authentic, credible, specific details
cons: May not be camera-ready, unpredictable
```

**Actors**:
```yaml
pros: Professional, reliable, good on camera
cons: Can feel fake if not done well
```

### 2. Scripted vs Unscripted

**Scripted**:
```yaml
pros: Controlled message, efficient
cons: Can sound rehearsed
approach: Write naturally, allow improvisation
```

**Unscripted**:
```yaml
pros: Authentic, genuine
cons: May ramble, need heavy editing
approach: Guide with questions, edit tightly
```

### 3. B-Roll Coverage

```yaml
while_talking:
  - Show product in use
  - Show results
  - Show lifestyle
  - Show before/after

purpose:
  - Visual interest
  - Demonstrate claims
  - Cover edit points
```

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

```yaml
mistake_1: Too salesy
fix: Let customer tell genuine story

mistake_2: Vague claims
fix: Use specific numbers and details

mistake_3: Too long
fix: Edit tightly, keep moving

mistake_4: Unrelatable person
fix: Choose customer similar to target audience

mistake_5: No proof
fix: Show before/after, results, evidence
```
